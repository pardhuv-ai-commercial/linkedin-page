Practitioner positions

How I see the landscape

Positions formed from working at the intersection of enterprise sales, technical architecture, and live AI deployment across APAC — not predictions, but convictions.

On agentic AI adoption
"The governance gap is the real blocker — not the technology"
72% of Singapore businesses plan to deploy agentic AI within two years. Only 14% have a mature governance model. The ones who get this right first will treat governance architecture as a competitive advantage, not a compliance checkbox.
On multi-party AI operations
"The Two-Laptop Era must end — it's an architecture problem, not a skills problem"
When a financial institution's AML model degrades, fixing it requires four organisations who don't share a communication platform, a security perimeter, or a knowledge base. Agentic AI is the integration layer that was always missing.
On human-AI collaboration
"The role is shifting from execution to oversight — and that's a harder, more strategic job"
AI agents don't replace business development. They change what BD professionals must be excellent at: defining the right questions, validating outputs against domain reality, and making judgment calls no model can be held accountable for.
On enterprise AI deployment
"Most failed AI projects are commercial architecture failures, not technology failures"
Gartner predicts 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027. Reasons: escalating costs, unclear value, inadequate risk controls. These are business problems. The path forward is designing commercial models as rigorously as technical architecture.
