The thesis

The gap between theory and production is where enterprise AI actually lives

Every AI project looks good on a vendor slide. Most fail quietly in production — not because the technology doesn't work, but because the architecture wasn't designed for the real conditions: multiple vendors, regulated data, fragmented communication platforms, and humans who have to be accountable for autonomous decisions.

This site publishes practitioner-grade research on that gap. Not hype. Not tutorials. Architecture decisions, governance frameworks, and the failure modes that nobody puts in their case studies — drawn from 17 years of BD across MNCs and startups in APAC, now amplified by a structured human-AI research practice.

Every piece of research here is produced with a coordinated team of specialised AI agents. I define the agenda, validate the claims, and remain the final editorial authority. The agents do the heavy lifting. The judgment is mine.
